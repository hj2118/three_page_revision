SOLUTION

What to Do in Pet Stores

Look for signs of ill health, such as listlessness, diarrhea, emaciation, dull coats, runny eyes, and dry noses. Check the general sanitation conditions; notice signs of cockroach infestation, rodent droppings on the floor, and rusty or dirty cages. Also look for water bottles that contain algae or scum, empty water containers, or animals who are having difficulty obtaining water.


What You Can Do

1) Report any sick or injured animal to your local animal control agency or police department immediately.

2) Contact PETA (People for the Ethical Treatment of Animals) about working on legislation to require that animals sold in stores be spayed or neutered.

3) Do not patronize pet stores that sell animals. Purchase supplies for companion animals from pet-supply stores or catalogs that carry full product lines but that do not treat animals as merchandise. Adopt animal companions from a reputable animal shelter or rescue organization.
